My research is concerned with the geometry (broadly construed) of sheaf-theoretic moduli spaces, especially their intersection theory.
Right now, my focus lies on the volumes of Quot schemes. This investigation is guided by two closely related examples.

On the one hand, the volume of the Grassmannian Gr(k,n) of k-dimensional subspaces in Cn is (k(n-k))! ∏ (j-1)!/(n-j)!, where the product is taken over all 1 ≤ j ≤ k. This formula, which is due to Schubert, also has an enumerative interpretation; in the simplest nontrivial case (n = 4, k = 2), it gives the number of lines in projective 3-space which meet four given generic lines.

On the other hand, consider a curve C of genus g, and the moduli space UC(r,O) of semistable vector bundles over C with rank r and trivial determinant. Witten gave a formula for the volume of UC(r,O) in terms of the volume of SU(r) and the series ∑ (dim ρ)2-2g, which is taken over the irreducible representations ρ of SU(r).

In a completely different direction, I am also interested in random matrix theory and time series analysis.
