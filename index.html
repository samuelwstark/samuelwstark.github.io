I am currently a Simons Foundation research fellow at the Isaac Newton Institute, University of Cambridge. Before that I was a postdoc of Rahul Pandharipande at ETH ZÃ¼rich, and a research student of Richard Thomas at Imperial College.

My research is concerned with the geometry of sheaf-theoretic moduli spaces, especially their intersection theory.
The example which guides, in one way or another, much of research in moduli theory is the Grassmannian — the moduli space of k-dimensional linear subspaces of n-dimensional projective space. Its intersection theory has intriguing connections to the combinatorics of Schur polynomials and the representation theory of the general linear group. The intersection theory of Grothendieck's Quot schemes, which are far-reaching generalisations of Grassmannians, is more difficult to study and promises to be very rich. Right now, I am computing the volumes of these schemes — this will lead to a generalisation of a well-known formula of Schubert; these volumes are also parellel to the volumes of the moduli spaces of semistable vector bundles, which were computed in a well-known paper of Witten.
